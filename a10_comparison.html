Here we have loaded each of the models in this new notebook, as well as the results from the cross validation analysis. By looking at the specific column where the parameters made the greatest impact (ie. most optimized), we can get a proper comparison between the scores of the simpler Gaussian Naive Bayes model and the more complex Decision Tree Classifier. We can see that the more complex model, DTC, has a mean_fit_time of almost half of the Gaussian Naive Bayes. This means that the Decision Tree Classification model performed its training/fitting in much less time. This reduction in time has not brought lower test scores, but in fact have resulted in increased accuracy, which is expected from the Decision Tree Classifier. While both models are not great at classification in this wine quality scenario, the Decision Tree Classifier is still much better in terms of both the accuracy score and the fit time. However, because they are both not reasonably effective in their task, we would recommend against the deployment of either model. However, it worth noting the increase effectiveness of the more complex Decision Tree Classification Model under optimized parameters.
