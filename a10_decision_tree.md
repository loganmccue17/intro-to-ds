Under a cross validation report, the Decision Tree gave an initial score of 0.525. This is not a great overall accuracy for our classification experiment in terms of projected subjective wine quality. When looking at our default classification report, the model was weak at classifying wines that were rated lower (3 or 4). This was likely due to a low count in these types of wines that contribute to our training data, meaning the model was not able to effectively predict these. Where there was a higher count of wines in a given category (for instance wine quality of 5 or 6), there was a much greater overall accuracy, precision, and recall. This means that the model was not only correct more often, but the model correctly identified the wine when both the predictions and actual labels were positive.

After optimization, the overall accuracy of 0.525 increased to 0.5875. Both of these accuracies are with a 5-fold evaluation. This means that the optimized parameters did see an increase in the projected accuracies, though still not very great. This probably could not be optimized much further without higher counts for the bounds of wine quality (5 or 8 eg.). Before overall deployment of this model, I would suggest an increase in training data for these edge cases. Otherwise, the model was fairly good at wine quality prediction, and was better under optimized paramaters (max_depth = 5, min_samples_split = 2, min_samples_leaf = 2).

Both models (default and optimized) do not classify wine quality with enough accuracy to justify its deployment. I would suggest the implementation of a more complicated model that may be able to account for these discrepancies. Otherwise, a larger dataset may be able to mitigate the model's innaccuracies. Similarly, some of these innacurracies could possibly be lowered by only focusing on a specific subset of attributes, instead of all of them. With tests performed regarding both of these suggestions, it is possible that this task is no better suited at classification than what was already analyzed and that there is enough overlap in its continuous attributes to make classification tricky.
